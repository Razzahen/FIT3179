World Happiness Ratings

Which Countries are the Happiest?

The map to the right can be used to check the happiness score for each country. We can see that Australia, Canada, Norway, Sweden, New Zealand and Iceland, are some of the highest scoring countries. While Zimbabwe, Kenya, Tanzania are some of the lower scoring in terms of happiness. Interestingly, countries in the same regions tend to have similar scores. But there are also some neighbouring countries which have significnatly different scores

How does GDP affect the Happiness Score?

The histogram to the right indicates that there is a positive correlation between GDP and happiness score. We can see that The countries with the higher GDP tend to have a higher happiness rating. This is expected, as countries with a higher GDP can afford to live a more prosperous life

The relationship between GDP, life expectancy and social support and their impact on happiness score?

From the scatterplot below, we can see that there is a strong positive correlation between GDP snd the other two variables. What is clear in all scatterplots is that happiness score is increased with increased health services and an increased life expectancy. Interestingly, there is also a positive relationship between Social support and life expectancy

How does GDP affect trust in the government and freedom of choice?

From the plots below we can see how increased GDP, results in higher levels of trust and freedom and consequently, a higher happiness score. The heatmap shows that while there is no clear trend with the low-mid GDP countries, the countries with a higher GDP have significantly more trust in the government.
